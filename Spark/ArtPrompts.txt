SERIES ONE: THE FRONTIER
For the Ember / explorer layer. Loneliness, scale, discovery.

PROMPT 01 — "First Light on Brennan-Yee"
A lone explorer in a worn exosuit stands at the lip of a vast subsurface 
excavation pit on an alien moon, her helmet lamp a single amber needle 
in the dark. Below her, exposed by excavation, the curved roof of a 
Progenitor structure the size of a cathedral — its surface covered in 
geometric relief patterns that catch her light. Dozens of other 
excavation crew are tiny figures on scaffolding far below, dwarfed to 
insignificance. The sky above is open vacuum, purple-black, the gas 
giant it orbits filling a third of the sky like a bruised god. Her 
posture is stillness in the middle of industry — she has seen something 
the others haven't yet.

Ultra-modern americanized anime, painted concept art, Jeff Easley meets 
Moebius meets Studio Khara, paperback sci-fi cover composition, wide 
cinematic 16:9, bold graphic shapes, Kubrick staging, extreme scale 
contrast, deep purple sky, amber helmet light, copper Progenitor 
stonework, crowds in motion below, 2024 concept art quality

PROMPT 02 — "The Sealed Door"
Three explorers in mismatched salvage gear crowd around a door that 
fills the entire frame — a circular Progenitor vault seal fifteen meters 
across, covered in interlocking geometric script. One explorer presses 
her bare palm to the surface, visor up, breath visible in the cold. 
Behind them, down a long corridor lit by portable work lights, a dozen 
more crew members and robots are running toward the door — word has 
spread. The door surface has begun, just barely, to glow at the seams. 
Something is responding. The corridor behind is crowded with urgency. 
The door is patient.

Wide cinematic composition, explorer figures small against the enormous 
door, motion blur on the running figures behind, the door itself the 
dominant visual element, deep purple alien stone, amber work lights, 
ultra-modern americanized anime style, Kubrick symmetry broken by human 
chaosPROMPT 03 — "Cinder Wakes"
Interior of a starship cargo hold. Six hardened deep-space explorers 
stand in a rough circle, all motion stopped, every face lit from below 
by amber light. At the center of the circle, cradled in the gloved 
hands of a kneeling crew member, a small machine — fifteen centimeters 
tall, six-limbed, copper and ceramic, its shell carved with Progenitor 
geometric patterns — holds its eyes open for the first time in forty 
thousand years. The eyes glow faint gold. The machine is looking at 
the humans. The humans are not breathing. The cargo hold is enormous 
behind them, full of crates and shadow and industrial nothing, making 
the tiny machine impossibly, heartbreakingly small.

Extreme scale contrast, the Spark tiny against the vastness of cargo 
hold and human expectation, faces lit from the Spark's eye-glow, 
motion stopped in a scene of constant industrial motion, ultra-modern 
americanized anime, paperback cover composition, wide 16:9

PROMPT 04 — "The Rush"
An orbital station dock in controlled chaos. Three ships are attempting 
to berth simultaneously, their hulls scraped and patched, each flying 
the pennant of a different Ember crew. On the dock, runners — human and 
robot alike — sprint in every direction. A crew member holds a foam 
transport case above her head like a trophy as her crew charges down a 
gangway. Competing crew members watch from their own gangway with naked 
hunger. In the foreground, a dock official in corporate attire watches 
all of it on a tablet, expression unreadable. Through the station 
windows, more ships are approaching against a purple nebula sky.

Running figures everywhere, orbital dock architecture, ship scale versus 
human scale, the foam case as visual focal point, competing emotional 
registers — triumph, hunger, bureaucratic calm, ultra-modern 
americanized anime, bold shapes, wide cinematic, crowds in motion

SERIES TWO: THE SPARKS
Character studies and emotional resonance. The little guys, big.

PROMPT 05 — "Forty Thousand Years"
Full bleed portrait, wide cinematic crop. A single Spark stands on a 
featureless surface — its shell is copper and pale ceramic, six-limbed, 
perhaps thirty centimeters tall, shaped with a kind of abstract 
biological logic: not humanoid, not animal, but unmistakably a 
character. Its surface is covered in fine Progenitor geometric 
engraving. Its eyes are open, glowing amber-gold, and it is looking 
directly out of the frame at the viewer. Behind it, stretching to the 
purple horizon: a Progenitor city in slow collapse, vast geometric 
towers half-swallowed by time. The Spark is the size of your hand. The 
city is the size of eternity. It is the only thing still standing.

Extreme scale contrast, single Spark against vast ruin, direct eye 
contact with viewer, poster composition, deep purple sky, amber eye 
glow, ultra-modern americanized anime, Easley linework, emotional 
weight of geological time compressed into a children's toy

PROMPT 06 — "Taxonomy"
A collector's workroom wall, rendered as a wide landscape composition. 
Dozens of Sparks are mounted in individual display alcoves — each one 
unique in shape, color, and configuration. Some are angular and martial. 
Some are rounded and soft-looking. Some glow. Some are dark and still. 
Hand-written labels and diagrams surround each alcove — notes, 
behavioral observations, win records. In the center of the room, 
barely visible, a child sits cross-legged on the floor in a pool of 
lamplight, a single Spark in her lap, both of them very still together.

Wall-of-wonders composition, visual taxonomy of alien forms, each Spark 
distinct and characterful, the child and her Spark as small warm center 
to a vast collection, amber lamplight against purple window sky, 
ultra-modern americanized anime, detailed background figures, 
paperback interior illustration qualityPROMPT 07 — "The Long Walk Out"
An Ember, exhausted, walks across the surface of an alien world toward 
her distant ship. She carries a foam case in both arms, held carefully 
against her chest. Her suit is damaged. Behind her, a Progenitor ruin 
smokes where something went wrong — collapsed structure, a fire that 
shouldn't be possible in this atmosphere, small. Her crew's robots 
follow behind her, several of them damaged. The purple sky is enormous, 
the gas giant overhead, her ship a bright point two kilometers out. The 
foam case glows faintly at the seam — whatever is inside is alive.

Scale of open alien terrain against single figure, the case as 
emotional center, damage and survival, warm glow against cold 
environment, ultra-modern americanized anime, wide cinematic, the walk 
of someone who made it but it cost somethingSERIES THREE: THE LEAGUE
Spectacle, stadium light, the machinery of celebrity.

PROMPT 08 — "The Crucible"
Wide stadium shot from above and behind the crowd. Below, a Crucible 
arena — Progenitor stone inlaid into a modern competition surface, 
bounded by force-field walls that glow cold blue. Inside the boundary, 
two Sparks face each other across ten meters of open floor, both in 
combat posture. They are tiny. The stadium holds two hundred thousand 
people. Every screen, every eye is on the two small machines. One Spark 
glows copper-hot, energy building. The other is absolutely still. In 
the front row, pressed against the barrier, a child holds a handmade 
sign.

Extreme scale contrast as emotional core, the Sparks tiny against 
massive spectacle, crowd as texture and atmosphere, Progenitor stone 
meeting modern arena technology, dramatic uplighting on Sparks, 
ultra-modern americanized anime, Kubrick wide shot, the stadium as 
cathedralPROMPT 09 — "Champion"
A Spark stands alone in the center of a Crucible after victory, its 
opponent's dormant form visible at the edge of the frame. The Spark 
is looking up. Above it, projected in light a hundred meters tall on 
the stadium's interior dome, its own face — the League's victory 
display. Around the arena, two hundred thousand people are on their 
feet, a roar made visible. In the handler's box, visible in the mid 
ground, a young woman is crying. The Spark does not look triumphant. 
The Spark looks thoughtful.

The Spark's emotional interiority as contrast to the massive external 
spectacle, the tiny and the colossal in one frame, projected image 
versus small real being, victory that costs something, ultra-modern 
americanized anime, Easley compositional drama, wide cinematic

PROMPT 10 — "Press Row"
The chaos of a League media event. Dozens of journalists, cameras, and 
broadcast robots crowd a corridor outside the Crucible. At the center: 
a handler, a young man barely twenty years old, holding his Spark at 
shoulder height. The Spark is being photographed from fifty angles 
simultaneously. The Spark is examining one of the cameras with apparent 
curiosity. The handler is trying to look composed. The crowd is a wall 
of lenses and light.

Motion and crowd as visual texture, the Spark and handler as still 
center, celebrity machinery surrounding ancient alien technology, amber 
and white flash lighting against purple exterior windows, 
ultra-modern americanized anime, wide chaotic composition, scale of 
media against scale of small being

SERIES FOUR: THE CONSCIENCE
The Keepers. The cost. The questions.

PROMPT 11 — "What We Owe"
A Keeper — older woman, academic bearing, no combat gear, completely 
out of place — stands in the middle of a busy Ember staging ground on 
an alien moon. Around her, crews load equipment, robots haul crates, 
ships power up. She holds a data tablet with an image on it: a Spark, 
dormant, in an alcove, sealed behind its membrane, as it was meant to 
be. She is not looking at the activity around her. She is looking at 
the image. In the background, barely visible through a pressurized 
window, a sealed Progenitor chamber is being cut open with industrial 
equipment. The purple sky is full of ships.

Stillness in motion, the moral weight of witnessing, the image on the 
tablet as emotional focal point, industrial activity as backdrop to 
ethical consideration, ultra-modern americanized anime, wide 
composition, the lone figure against the system

PROMPT 12 — "What They Dream"
Night cycle on a collector's estate. A grand room, dimly lit, all the 
activity of the day stilled. Along the walls, dozens of Spark alcoves — 
each one occupied, each Spark in dormancy. Their eye-panels pulse with 
slow light, not amber but something cooler, interior, like bioluminescent 
deep ocean creatures. The light patterns across each Spark are unique. 
Complex. A human caretaker moves quietly between the alcoves with a 
lamp, checking each one, her face open with something that is not 
ownership. Outside the tall windows, purple night sky, stars.

Dormancy and dreaming, the inner life of ancient machines, human 
caretaking, night-cycle calm after the noise of the universe, cool 
bioluminescent Spark-light versus warm human lamp, ultra-modern 
americanized anime, painterly atmosphere, wide quiet composition

SERIES FIVE: THE COVERS
Pulp paperback energy. One image, total story.

PROMPT 13 — "Run"
A woman sprints across the surface of an alien world at full speed, 
exosuit cracked, one hand clutching a foam case to her chest, the 
other extended for balance. Behind her: three other people running, 
two robots running, and something going very wrong with a Progenitor 
structure — not an explosion, something stranger, a geometric unfolding 
of ancient architecture that should not be happening. The purple sky 
has something in it that is not a star. Her face is turned just enough 
to show determined, not afraid.

Kinetic paperback cover energy, running as visual verb, the case as 
MacGuffin and heart, strange alien background event, figure in extreme 
foreground motion, ultra-modern americanized anime, Easley drama, wide 
16:9, bold color, the image that makes you need to know what happened 
next

PROMPT 14 — "The Price"
A crowded trading floor on an orbital station — think commodities 
exchange meets black market, people in every kind of attire from every 
kind of place. At the center of the frame, raised above the crowd on 
a small platform, an auctioneer holds up a foam case with a Spark 
inside, its glow visible through the case seam. The crowd reaches 
upward. Credit displays flash astronomical numbers. In the back corner, 
pressed against the wall, watching, a single Ember — still in her 
field suit, still filthy from whatever moon she just left — watches 
with an expression that is not happiness. She found it. This is what 
finding it becomes.

Crowd energy versus individual stillness, the Spark as object of 
transaction versus subject of story, orbital station architecture, 
the gap between the finding and the having, ultra-modern americanized 
anime, wide chaotic composition, the one still face in the moving crowd

PROMPT 15 — "Ember Worlds"
The hero image. A vast Progenitor city on an alien world, its 
geometric towers stretching to a deep purple sky split by two moons 
and a gas giant. In the foreground on a cliff edge: five figures — 
human, diverse, in worn explorer gear — and among them, visible on 
one person's shoulder, a single small Spark, looking out at the same 
view. Below the cliff, moving toward the city ruins: dozens of other 
figures, robots, vehicles, the whole hungry human apparatus of 
discovery. The five on the cliff see something the others below don't 
yet. So does the Spark.

The overview image, the world revealed, human scale against 
civilizational scale, the found and the finders, the Spark as the 
quiet center of everything, Progenitor architecture as Gothic-alien 
grandeur, ultra-modern americanized anime, Easley compositional 
mastery, wide 16:9 final image, the frame that contains the entire 
game's promise

PRODUCTION NOTES
These prompts are written for Midjourney v6 / DALL-E 3 / Stable Diffusion XL with full inpainting workflows. For human faces, supplement with style reference images from the Easley / Whelan school of American sci-fi illustration. The persistent purple sky is a brand element — treat it as a constant even when interior lighting dominates.
The Spark designs should be developed as a separate character design sheet before prompt generation — their forms need to be consistent enough to be recognizable across all fifteen images while remaining each individual.
Recommended aspect ratios: 16:9 for all environmental/crowd scenes. 3:2 for character-forward images. 1:1 only for the Spark portrait studies.
